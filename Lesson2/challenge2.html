LESSON CHALLENGE

TYPESET A POEM BY THE METAPHYSICAL ADVENTURESS

WHAT TO DO:

One of my favorite poets is the marvelous Emily Dickinson. I love to fantasize about
her shut inside of her Massachusetts home, wearing white ruffly shirts, and wirting
impossibly romantic poetry.

In honor of Emily, copy and past the following poem into the code editor and use
the HTML tags you now know to typeset it. Use headlines where appropriate, tags if you like'em and feel free to throw a break or em tag in there where
appropriate.

Ah, and since Ms. Dickinson was so fond of them ... you can use the (HTML) special
character for those famous em dashes which is (—) no greater than or less
than symbols required. Learn more about special characters.

A Coffin-is a Small Domain

A Coffin-is a small Domain,
Yet able to contain
A Citizen of Paradise
In it diminished Plane.

A Grave-is a restricted Breadth-
Yet ampler than the Sun-
And all the Seas He populates
And Lands He looks upon

To Him who onits small Repose
Bestows a single Fried-
Circumference without Relief-
Or Estimate-or End-

Emily Dickinson
